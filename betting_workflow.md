# Betting Workflow

This is the operating playbook for daily MLB betting decisions. It should reflect durable rules developed in the Betting Strategy thread.

## Purpose

Use a repeatable process to decide whether a game is a pregame bet, a live-watch candidate, or a pass. The dashboard is a decision-support tool, not an automatic betting system.

## Daily workflow

### 1. Select games for deeper review

Prioritize games with one or more of the following:

- Meaningful starting-pitcher mismatch
- Questionable market pricing
- Bullpen availability edge
- Strong lineup or handedness split
- Useful live-betting setup

### 2. Pregame review

#### Starting pitching

Review:

- ERA, FIP, xFIP, xERA, WHIP
- K-BB%
- Hard-hit% and barrel%
- Recent form
- Pitch count and workload trends
- Velocity and pitch-mix changes

#### Offense

Review:

- Recent performance against the starter's handedness
- wRC+, OPS, ISO
- Strikeout% and walk%
- Official lineup and missing hitters

#### Bullpen

Review:

- Previous three days of usage
- High-leverage reliever availability
- Rest and back-to-back appearances

#### Market

Review:

- Opening line and current line
- No-vig probability
- Estimated fair probability
- F5 price versus full-game price
- Whether the edge remains after line movement

### 3. Classify the game

Use one of three labels:

- **BET:** The baseball thesis and market price both support an entry.
- **WATCH:** The matchup is promising, but live confirmation is needed.
- **PASS:** The edge is too small, uncertain, or already priced in.

### 4. Live-game review

Monitor:

- Pitch count and pitches per inning
- Strike percentage and walks
- Velocity trend
- Strikeouts, whiffs, and command
- Exit velocity and hard-hit contact
- Starter times-through-the-order risk
- Bullpen readiness
- Current live price versus updated fair price

### 5. Record the decision

Every wager should eventually include:

- Game and market
- Entry odds
- Stake
- Reason for entry
- Fair-price estimate
- Closing line
- Result
- Closing-line value
- Postgame lesson

## Guardrails

- Do not bet solely because one team is better.
- Do not chase a price after the original edge disappears.
- Separate F5 analysis from full-game analysis.
- Treat small live samples as confirmation signals, not complete proof.
- Prefer a pass over forcing action.
